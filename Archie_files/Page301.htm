AMA Computer Learning Center og Naga City started last February 26, 1996 on its temporary site at Manubay Building along Peñafrancia Ave., Naga City beside Emily Theater. The Center offers short-term budget courses in DOS, Word processing, Electronic Spreadsheet and Database Management. Twenty units of 486 DX4 microcomputers and Initial training was conducted by its School Director Mr. Lamberto E. Pullantes and its Education Coordinator, Ms. Melinda Caresosa. Opening of classes begun on June 15, !996 with 367 first year  students, enrolled in two year programs such as Computer System Design and Programming, Computer System Technician and Computer Secretarial Business option. Additional 20 units of microcomputers were instead in the center to cater the needs of the growing population.

 

After 5 months stay on its temporary site, October 6, 1996 where AMA-CLC transferred to its newly constructed permanent site at 3rd floor BOOKSHOP Center p. Burgos cor. Barlin St., Naga City. Its Managing Director Maria Co  Say Litam, decided to open additional laboratories to ensure one computer per student hands–on, training. As a challenged to the growth of information technology,  AMA-CLC includes internet subjects on its new curriculum and allow internet access to every student. Present students can access to the world of information via internet, sub-surfing, electronic mail, internet relay chat and other online services.
